Metamo turns off-the-shelf LLM into empathetic coach using single-pass wrapper that identifies cognitive distortion behind an emotion, then recognizes user's emotion and generates empathetic Socratic (question-centered) response that invites reflection all within one model call.

Existing systems use single response that combines both perception and advice. This has two limitations: Generic response without empathy because of lack of context/"why" user feels that way and multi-prompt calling pushes response latency beyond natural window of 5 seconds.

Negative emotions are results of recurring thought patterns known as cognitive distortions. Distortion detection is fundamental procedure in CBT and improves LLM reasoning. According to CBT, mental disorders are closely associated to maladaptive thought patterns that manifest through emotional responses. For example: depressed and/or anxious person will have negative thoughts that will lead to negative emotions.

## 1) Distortion detection

Distortion detection is divided into 3 steps: subjectivity assessment, contrastive reasoning and schema analysis. 

#### 1. Subjectivity assessment 
On this step, we identify objective facts and subjective thoughts/opinions. 

#### 2. Contrastive reasoning
On this step, we construct two distinct reasoning processes to evaluate subjective thoughts using objective evidence (one that supports their thoughts while the other contradicts them)

#### 3. Schema analysis
A "schema" acts as a mental blueprint combining personal knowledge, beliefs, and expectations to interpret situations coherently. In this method, schema analysis reveals how these structures lead to cognitive distortions—irrational thinking errors linked to negative emotions. In other words, it identifies the root cognitive model driving the distortion, enabling large language models (LLMs) to classify it into one of 10 types from CBT research: **personalization** (blaming yourself excessively), **mind reading** (assuming others' thoughts), **overgeneralization** (one event defines all), **all-or-nothing thinking** (extremes only), **emotional reasoning** (feelings as facts), **labeling** (name-calling self/others), **magnification** (exaggerating negatives), **mental filter** (ignoring positives), **should statements** (rigid rules), and **fortune-telling** (predicting doom)[file:1]

## 2) Emotion recognition

Emotion recognition follows distortion detection, using the built cognitive model to predict emotions in the conversation (or specific utterances) more deeply than basic sentiment analysis that only looks at surface words.

#### Chain of emotions
The "chain of emotions" tracks emotional flow between speaker/listener across dialogue turns, directly linked to detected distortions. It bridges deep cognitive thinking (distorted schemas) to surface emotional expression for nuanced understanding.

**Example** (train anxiety from paper):  
- Speaker: "I was so scared of the train! I hate fast-moving objects." → **Anxious** (emotional reasoning distortion)  
- Listener: "Was it your first time?" → **Caring**  
- Speaker: "No, but I still hate it." → **Anxious**  
**Chain**: Anxious → Caring → Anxious  
**Overall emotion**: **Anxious** [file:1]

**Why better**: Basic sentiment sees "scared/hate" = negative. Metamo sees specific distortion + emotional progression = understands *why* anxious, generates better coaching response.

## 3) Empathetic Response Generation
Uses situation + cognitive indicators (distortions) + chain of emotions to generate Socratic question + brief reframing suggestion. Keeps responses empathetic, reflective, and under 2-second latency.[file:1]
